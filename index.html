Hi! I'm a cognitive scientist at the University of Oxford, where I study human motivation and its connection to learning and decision making.

I use a mix of video game datasets and behavioural experiments, together with computational modelling tools.

About me

I'm a PostDoc with Christopher Summerfield and hold a Junior Research Fellowship at Linacre College in Oxford. Here I'm working on understanding people's motivations.

Before Oxford, I completed my PhD at the Max Planck Institute for Biological Cybernetics with Eric Schulz in Tübingen, Germany.

I'm interested in understanding people's intrinsic motivations. Which tasks attract us to spend our free time on? Why do we keep engaging with tasks that frustrate us? Why do we start activities that will leave us unsatisfied afterwards? And why are we sometimes avoiding effort, while at other times we actively seek it out?

In my work, I use large-scale video game datasets to find answers to these and similar questions.

During my PhD, we for example analyzed the data of 30.000 players of the game Little Alchemy to understand people's exploration behaviour.

In my time at Oxford, I developed a slingshot game inspired by Angry Birds to look in more detail at the interplay between motivation, learning and feedback.

In other work, we are looking at people's natural browsing behaviour, to understand the influence of different websites on multiple measures of intrinsic motivation.

My research has among others been featured in the German TV-show SWR Science Talk, in the newspaper Stuttgarter Zeitung, and in the podcast Behind the Screens.
